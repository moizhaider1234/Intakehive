Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I qualify for a mass tort claim?+
Qualification depends on the specific case type. Generally, you must have been exposed to the product or drug in question and have a documented medical diagnosis linked to that exposure. Fill out the eligibility form for your case type above — our team will review your details and contact you within 24–48 hours to discuss your options.
How much does it cost to pursue a claim?+
Nothing upfront. We work on a contingency fee basis, which means you pay nothing unless and until you receive compensation. There is no risk to you for submitting a claim review or speaking with our team.
How long do I have to file a claim?+
Each state has its own "statute of limitations" that limits how long you have to file a lawsuit after an injury. The window varies by case type and state — typically 2 to 4 years from the date of diagnosis or discovery of harm. Don't delay: contact us as soon as possible to preserve your rights.
What is a mass tort lawsuit?+
A mass tort involves many individuals harmed by the same product, drug, or action by a company. Unlike a class action, each plaintiff maintains an individual case and potential compensation amount. Mass torts allow injured individuals to take on large corporations more effectively by sharing legal resources and evidence.
How long does a mass tort claim take?+
Timelines vary significantly. Some cases settle within 1–2 years; others, particularly those in active federal MDL proceedings, may take 3–5 years or longer. Your attorney will keep you updated throughout the process. Many cases settle before going to trial, which can speed up resolution.
What compensation might I receive?+
Potential compensation varies widely by case type, severity of injury, and other factors. You may be entitled to medical expenses (past and future), lost wages and lost earning capacity, pain and suffering, and in some cases, punitive damages against the negligent company. Notable verdicts in our active campaigns have ranged from tens of millions to billions of dollars.
What if I already have an attorney?+
If you already have legal representation for your specific claim, we generally cannot take on your case. However, if you have not yet retained an attorney for your particular mass tort claim, we encourage you to reach out to explore your options. There is no obligation in doing so.
Is my information kept confidential?+
Yes. All information you submit is protected by attorney-client privilege and our strict privacy policy. Your personal and medical information is never sold or shared with unauthorized third parties. All submissions are SSL encrypted for your protection.
